# LinkedIn Outreach Messages

Three messages to send across the summer. The goal is a real conversation with someone doing
work I find interesting — not to ask for a job.

---

## Message 1 — Week 2: Real-time systems / developer tools engineer

**Who to find:** Search LinkedIn for "software engineer socket.io", "staff engineer developer
tools", "engineer real-time systems". Look at their posts/articles first.

**Template (personalise the bracketed parts):**

> Hi [Name], I'm a second-year computer engineering student. I read your post about [specific
> thing they wrote — a post about distributed systems, a talk they gave, an article] and I'm
> curious — [one specific technical question, e.g. "when you're building a real-time feature,
> how do you decide between WebSockets and long polling in practice?"]. No obligation at all,
> just genuinely curious.

**My filled-in version (example):**

> Hi Sarah, I'm a second-year computer engineering student. I read your post about building
> collaborative features at [Company] and I'm curious — when you moved from polling to
> WebSockets, did you have to change how you thought about server-side state, or was the socket
> layer mostly an infrastructure detail? No obligation at all, just building something similar
> and want to understand the tradeoffs better.

---

## Message 2 — Week 5: Graphics / game engine engineer

**Who to find:** Search "graphics engineer", "game engine developer", "rendering engineer",
"GPU programming". Companies: Epic Games, Unity, Valve, NVIDIA, any game studio.

**Template:**

> Hi [Name], I'm a second-year computer engineering student. I've been working through the
> Ray Tracing in One Weekend series this summer and just got to the Monte Carlo importance
> sampling section. I'm curious — in your day-to-day work on [game engine / rendering system],
> how much of this low-level rendering knowledge actually shows up? Or is most of it abstracted
> by the engine? No obligation at all.

---

## Message 3 — Week 8: Engineer at a company I'd actually want to work at

**Who to find:** Find someone at a company I'm excited about. Look at their GitHub, their
posts, something they've actually shipped. The message should reference something specific.

**Template:**

> Hi [Name], I'm a second-year computer engineering student. I've been following [Company]'s
> engineering blog and found [specific post/talk] really interesting — especially [specific
> technical detail]. I'm curious: [one specific question about their technical decisions, not
> about the company's culture or process]. No obligation at all.

---

## Notes

- Keep it to one message. If they don't reply in 2 weeks, don't follow up. Move on.
- If they do reply, have a real conversation. Ask a follow-up question based on what they said.
- Don't ask for a referral or internship in the first message. Build the relationship first.
- The subject line matters on LinkedIn — it shows up as a preview. Keep it direct:
  "Question about [technical topic]" works better than "Hi from a student!"
