# Personal Pitch — "Tell me about yourself"

Write this out. Practise saying it out loud. Record yourself. Fix what sounds awkward.
Target: 60 seconds when spoken.

---

## Draft (Week 9)

I'm a second-year computer engineering student at [University]. This summer I spent three
months building serious projects from scratch — a real-time collaborative code review tool
with WebSockets and a full authentication system, a physically-based ray tracer in C++ that
implements Monte Carlo importance sampling, and a neural network trained from scratch in
NumPy without using any ML libraries. I also contributed a bug fix to socket.io that got
merged into their production codebase.

I'm most interested in the intersection of systems and software — understanding how things
actually work at a low level while still building products people use. That's what drew me to
networks and graphics alongside the full-stack work. I'm looking for a role where I can keep
going deep on technical problems, ideally at a company building developer tools, infrastructure,
or anything with interesting real-time or systems challenges.

---

## Notes for delivery

- Don't rush. Speak slightly slower than you think you need to.
- The part about "this summer I built..." is where interviewers lean in — make it concrete.
- Don't say "I'm passionate about" — say what you actually built and why.
- End on what you're looking for, not on a vague "and that's me!" — it gives them somewhere
  to take the conversation.
- Record yourself on your phone, listen back, identify the one phrase that sounds most awkward,
  rewrite that phrase, repeat.

---

## For different interview contexts

**For a systems/infra role:** emphasise the ray tracer (C++, performance, low-level) and the
packet analyser (C, libpcap, network protocols).

**For an ML/AI role:** emphasise the neural network from scratch (backpropagation, NumPy,
MNIST 97.8% accuracy) and that you can implement, not just call, ML primitives.

**For a full-stack/product role:** emphasise the code review tool (deployed, live, end to end).

**For a security role:** emphasise the CTF work, the packet analyser, and that you understand
vulnerabilities from building both the exploit and the target.
